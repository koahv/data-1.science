#### Extract
>(Wyss Institute for Biologically Inspired Engineering at Harvard) In a study published on May 23 in Nature Methods, a Wyss Institute team reports how it rigorously compared and ranked the most commonly used artificial Cas9 activators in different cell types from organisms including humans, mice and flies. The findings provide a valuable guide to researchers, allowing them to streamline their endeavors.
