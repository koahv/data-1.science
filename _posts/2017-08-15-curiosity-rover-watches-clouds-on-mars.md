#### Extract
>Barely visible water-ice clouds coast across Mars’s skies in new videos from the Curiosity rover. Thin clouds float above NASA’s Curiosity Mars Rover last July. This videos is made from eight still images each taken over a period of four minutes by Curiosity’s Navcam camera. NASA / JPL-Caltech / York University Every now and then, the operators of NASA’s Mars Science Laboratory mission Curiosity take a break from avoiding sand traps and analyzing rocks for signs of ancient, habitable environments and instead point the rover’s robotic eyes to the sky to look at the clouds. That’s what they did last month on July 17th, when early in the Martian morning they pointed Curiosity’s Navigation Camera (Navcam) up to capture two image sequences of feathery clouds as they drifted overhead. The first clip shows smoky thin clouds moving from left to right as they pass right above the rover.
