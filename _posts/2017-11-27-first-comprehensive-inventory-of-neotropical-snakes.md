#### Digest
>An international team made up of scientists from Brazil, Australia, the U.S., Ecuador, Germany and Sweden has published the results of an extensive database of snakes of the American tropics. The database will serve as a solid basis for conservation concepts, and biodiversity and evolution models in the future. The results from this most comprehensive and novel database also highlight the necessity to better sample, explore, and protect areas of high diversity, as well as rare species.



#### Factsheet
>There are around 10,500 species of reptiles around the world with snakes making up around 34% of that group.
